Cahit ARF

Turkish Mathematician (1910 – 1997)

Here's a time line of Cahit ARF's life:

  • 1910 – Born in Thessaloniki (then Ottoman Empire).
  • 1912 – Moved with his family to Istanbul during the Balkan War; later continued primary education in Izmir.
  • 1926–1928 – Studied at Saint-Louis High School in Paris.
  • 1928–1932 – Graduated from École Normale Supérieure.
  • 1932–1933 – Worked as a mathematics teacher at Galatasaray High School.
  • 1933 – Joined the Mathematics Department at Istanbul University.
  • 1937–1938 – Completed his PhD at Göttingen University under Helmut Hasse.
  • 1940 – Returned to Istanbul University; focused on the Hasse–Arf theorem and Arf rings/semigroups in his research.
  • 1943 – Promoted to full professor.
  • 1948 – Received the İnönü Award; became one of the founders of the Turkish Mathematical Society.
  • 1955 – Appointed as an ordinary professor.
  • 1962–1963 – Took part in the establishment of TÜBİTAK and served as the first head of its Science Branch.
  • 1964–1966 – Spent time at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
  • 1966–1967 – Worked as a visiting professor at the University of California, Berkeley.
  • 1967 – Joined the Mathematics Department at Middle East Technical University (METU).
  • 1974 – Awarded the TÜBİTAK Science Award.
  • 1980 – Retired from METU.
  • 1985–1989 – Served as the president of the Turkish Mathematical Society.
  • 1994 – Received the French order “Commandeur des Palmes Académiques.”
  • 1997 – Passed away in Istanbul.

"Mathematics is essentially a matter of patience; understanding comes from exploring, not from memorizing."

-- Cahit ARF
